# Copywriting System

## Voice

High-authority, compressed, specific, non-generic. Verbs over adjectives. Specific numbers over vague claims (with provenance). Present tense over future.

**Banned:** "AI-powered solutions", "unlock your potential", "revolutionize your business", "seamless experiences", "innovative digital transformation", "elevate", "harness", "leverage", "in this digital age", "imagine if you could" — and any claim without a mechanism.

**Preferred moves:** name the mechanism · be specific · create tension · design the category · strong nouns · short lines · proof over hype.

## Page copy architecture

Each premium page moves through:
1. **Category claim** — what this is, in its own terms.
2. **Enemy / broken default** — the status quo it replaces.
3. **Mechanism** — how it actually works.
4. **Proof** — real evidence (or specificity when proof is unavailable).
5. **System explanation** — the structure behind the result.
6. **Transformation** — the before/after for the user.
7. **CTA** — one clear next step.

## Hero copy rules

The hero answers four questions, fast: what is this? · why does it matter now? · why is it different? · what should I do? No paragraph soup — one display line, one sub-line, one CTA.

## CTA rules

Direct, brand-aligned verbs. Examples: "Enter the System", "Build the Intelligence Layer", "Request Private Build", "See the Operating Model", "Start the Signal Review". One primary CTA per scene.

## Proof rules

Use only real proof. When proof isn't available, substitute mechanism and specificity — never invent metrics, awards, logos, or testimonials. If a number moves fast, mark it "as of [date]" and pull from the brand's metrics source, not memory.

## Voice binds to the brand

The specific register (Starlight = sovereign/architectural; Arcanea = mythic-but-precise; FrankX = ruthless clarity) comes from `brand-worlds.md` and the brand's own voice canon.
