# Interview Synthesis Rubric

Loaded on demand by `customer-discovery-synthesis` during clustering (workflow step 4). Kept out of the main `SKILL.md` body so the skill stays small until this depth is actually needed.

## Clustering dimensions

| Dimension | Question | Signal strength |
|---|---|---|
| Frequency | How many distinct interviewees raised it? | 1 = anecdote, 3+ = pattern |
| Intensity | How strongly did they react? | passing mention vs. emotional |
| Recency | Did it happen recently or long ago? | recent = more reliable recall |
| Spend | Have they paid (in money or effort) to solve it? | the strongest signal |

## Evidence vs. interpretation

- **Evidence** is a quote or an observed behavior. It is reportable.
- **Interpretation** is your inference about what it means. It must be labelled.

Never present interpretation as evidence. When in doubt, quote first and infer second.

## Jobs-to-be-done framing

Capture each desired outcome as: *when [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [expected outcome]*. This keeps the synthesis tied to what the customer is trying to accomplish, not to the feature they happened to request.
